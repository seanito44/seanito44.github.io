APHELION
for you

Underneath the starry sky, getting darker,
Here I write the loneliest kind of mathematics.
“Aphelion”, not quite capturing the feeling
Of being untethered, unfound, unknown.

Each day is a shorter one. And somehow longer. Tedious.
My sun, visible for a moment, before leaving the world
And I, cradling air, settle for vapid sleep
Until I can see her again.

Laying in a thick pool of outskirts’ swirling silence
I think. And drift into dreams
Where I can talk more and more, loud enough
That the words reach her somehow.

And feel her glow across my skin
That melts away the miles between.

Then I wake up. Cold sweat, hot pillow. Dead night.
But I look out the window anyway, and see it:
The moon, with a radiance, a brilliance
That only she could give, even when she’s far away.

Underneath the violet sky, I ponder the word
“Aphelion”, not quite capturing the excitement
Of the moment before coming home.
And I onward, gently drawn by patient hands, these words suffice:

Still I feel her warmth, still I see her light,
Still I smell her precious petals in angelic white
Still I hear her sweetest songs that cure the sorrow sighs
Still, I learn to see the world through her sunny eyes.

Still, I learn to see the world in her sunny eyes.

And somehow, in all this space — I found you.
